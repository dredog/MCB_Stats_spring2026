Player Rating Algorithm

MCB Summer 9U Baseball — Player Rating Algorithm

gc_MCB_9U_scraper.py · Last updated June 2026

Philosophy

Every player receives up to three component scores (0–100 scale), then a weighted Overall Score that blends them. The weights shift automatically for each player based on how much they've pitched — a kid who barely pitched isn't penalized by a weak pitching score; that weight silently redistributes to hitting and fielding instead.

All scores are scraped from GameChanger. No manual overrides are currently active (the DEFENSE and ALLSTAR dicts are empty for the summer 9U tool).

Teams Covered

MCB (Murphey Candler Baseball): Gold Sox, Blue Sox, White Sox, Red Sox

Buckhead Baseball: Bucks, Braves, Bulldogs, Bombers, Banditos

Component 1 — Hitting Score (0–100)

Minimum to qualify: 4 plate appearances (PA).

Formula:

sb_scale   = min(12.0 / G, 1.0)              (where G = games player has played)
sb_contrib = min(SB × 0.3 × sb_scale, 2.5)   (only for SB-tracked teams; else 0)
hit_raw    = AVG×35 + ISO×25 + OBP×12 + BB%×5 − K%×20 + sb_contrib
hit_score  = (hit_raw / 62.8) × 100 × pa_mult
Component Weight Notes
AVG 35 Backbone of the score
ISO (SLG−AVG) 25 Extra-base power
OBP 12 Gets on base, all methods
BB% 5 Low — at 9U, encourage attacking, not walking
K% −20 Penalty — contact and aggression rewarded
SB (count) 0.3/steal, cap 2.5 Tiny tiebreaker; see SB notes below

HIT_IDEAL = 62.8 (ideal player: .800 AVG, .800 ISO, .900 OBP, 30% BB, 0% K, SB at cap)

SB treatment in detail:

  • Raw count, not rate — total steals, not steals per PA, so players aren't penalized for having fewer plate appearances.
  • Scaled by games played — sb_scale = min(12/G, 1.0). Teams that have played more than 12 games get a proportionally smaller per-steal bonus, preventing seasonal accumulation from creating an unfair advantage over teams with fewer games. Teams with ≤12 games get the full 0.3/steal rate.
  • Capped at 2.5 raw points — approximately 9 unscaled steals (less after the games-played scale) to hit the ceiling. This caps the maximum SB contribution at 2.5/62.8 = ~4% of the ideal score. The intent is a very modest acknowledgment that counting bases matters, not a meaningful score driver, since at 9U most stolen bases occur on wild pitches or coach decisions rather than individual player speed.

Sample-size multiplier: pa_mult = 0.75 + 0.25 × min(PA/40, 1.0) — full credit at 40 PA.

Component 2 — Pitching Score (0–100)

Minimum to qualify: 1 out recorded AND ERA must be computable.

Why BF-denominated rates: K/6, BB/6, H/6, and ERA are all denominated by innings (outs recorded). At 9U, when defense is poor, errors extend at-bats without recording outs — a nominal "6 innings" can require 10+ batters faced. Switching K, BB, and H to per batter faced (BF) removes that distortion entirely.

BF source: Exact BF for Red Sox from GC screenshot; others estimated as outs + H + BB (within ±6 for all Red Sox pitchers, no systematic bias).

Normalization ranges: | Rate | Floor (scores 0) | Ceiling (scores 100) | |---|---|---| | STR% | 33% | 67% | | K% | 10% of BF | 50% of BF | | BB% | 35% of BF | 5% of BF | | H% | 40% of BF | 5% of BF |

Formula:

pit_raw   = STR_n×40 + H%_n×30 + K%_n×15 + BB%_n×10 + ERA_n×5
ip_mult   = 0.75 + 0.25 × min(outs/36, 1.0)
pit_score = clamp(1.6 × pit_raw × ip_mult − 7.0, 0, 100)

The 1.6× recalibration boost lifts the realistic 9U range (which clusters low due to sloppy defense and high walk rates) into a more readable spread. ERA is down-weighted to 5 since earned/unearned scorekeeping is unreliable at this age.

Pitching Confidence (BF-based)

Full-confidence threshold: 2.0 × team_games BF. For a 14-game season: 28 BF.

if BF < 5:        bf_conf = 0.0    (pitching not counted in OVR)
if BF ≥ 5:        bf_conf = 0.30 + 0.70 × min((BF−5) / (bf_full−5), 1.0)

At BF=5 (minimum): bf_conf = 0.30 → effective pitching weight = 27% × 0.30 = 8.1%

Any pitching weight not earned by BF confidence redistributes to hitting and fielding proportionally to their 58:15 base ratio. OVR always sums to 100%.

Example — Thomas B (10 BF, 14-game season, bf_full=28):

bf_conf    = 0.30 + 0.70 × (10−5)/(28−5) = 0.452
eff_pit_wt = 27% × 0.452 = 12.2%
eff_hit_wt = 58% + 14.8% × (58/73) = 69.8%
eff_fld_wt = 15% + 14.8% × (15/73) = 18.0%

Component 3 — Fielding Score (0–100)

Minimum to qualify: 1 total PO+A across any position.

Per-position quality

raw     = PO × PO_W[pos] + A × A_W[pos] − E × 0.3
quality = max(0, raw / chances / 4.5)

Position weights: | Position | PO weight | Assist weight | |---|---|---| | C | 0.2 | 2.0 | | 1B | 1.0 | 2.5 | | P | 1.5 | 2.0 | | 2B | 2.5 | 2.5 | | 3B | 2.5 | 3.5 | | SS | 2.5 | 3.5 | | LF / RF | 3.2 | 2.7 | | CF | 3.5 | 3.0 |

Catcher PO weight is near-zero (0.2) because K catches are routine. Outfield POs are highest (3.2–3.5) because fly ball catches are genuinely rare and difficult at 9U.

Outfield minimum filter: OF positions require at least 3 PO+A to count in the combined score. Fewer than 3 catches is too small a sample given the high PO_W value.

Per-position reliability

pos_rel = 0.30 + 0.70 × min(chances / full_ch, 1.0)
Position type Full confidence at
C and 1B 15 chances
All other positions 10 chances

Multiplicative combination + overall reliability

combined    = 1 − ∏(1 − quality × pos_rel)
overall_rel = 0.30 + 0.70 × min(total_PO_A / 8, 1.0)
field_score = combined × 100 × overall_rel

Overall Score (OVR)

Base weights: Hitting 58%, Pitching 27%, Fielding 15%

OVR is renormalized over whichever components have data — a player with no meaningful pitching still gets a full OVR from hitting + fielding.

Pitching Boost by Age Group

Age Boost formula Rationale
9U ×1.6 − 7 Most defense inflation; raw scores cluster lowest
10U ×1.5 − 6
11U ×1.4 − 5
12U ×1.3 − 4 Cleanest defense; raw scores already higher

All other constants are identical across age groups.
